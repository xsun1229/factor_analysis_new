Highlight:

  • Ubiquitin-mediated proteolysis and LDL cholesterol

ref: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0962892423001927

  • Other O-glycan biosynthesis and LDL cholesterol

ref: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4067166/

The non-mucin O-glycans in this pathway include:

  • O-fucose glycosylation (e.g., NOTCH signaling)
  • O-mannose glycosylation (α-dystroglycan)
  • O-GlcNAc modification
  • O-linked glycans on LDLR and other lipoprotein receptors

These modifications directly affect:

  • LDLR stability
  • LDLR recycling
  • LDL particle clearance
  • Apolipoprotein glycosylation (ApoE, ApoC-III)

Changes in these O-glycans alter plasma LDL levels and cholesterol homeostasis.
